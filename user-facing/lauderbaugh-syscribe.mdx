\# Onboarding Review Step: Confirm Your Documentation Setup ## What's New When connecting a new repository, you now go through a guided \*\*confirmation step\*\* before your documentation setup is finalized. After the initial scan of your repository, you'll land on a review screen where you can check what was found, correct any mistakes, and approve the setup before anything is committed. In addition, the repository settings panel now includes a \*\*Rescan\*\* option, letting you trigger a fresh scan of your repository at any time without leaving the settings page. --- ## Reviewing and Confirming Your Repository Scan After your repository is scanned during onboarding, you'll be taken to a confirmation screen. Here's what you can do on that screen: ### 1. Review Discovered Content You'll see a list of pages and files that were found in your repository. For each item, you can: - \*\*Check the document type\*\* — Each item is automatically classified as one of the following: - \*\*Internal\*\* — Documentation meant for your team only - \*\*User-Facing\*\* — Documentation for your end users - \*\*API\*\* — Technical reference documentation for developers - \*\*Change the document type\*\* — If the automatic classification is wrong, use the dropdown next to any item to select the correct type. - \*\*Exclude an item\*\* — If a file shouldn't be included in your documentation at all, you can toggle it to exclude it from the setup. ### 2. Review Flagged Issues If the scan detected anything that may need your attention (such as potential misclassifications or missing content), those issues will be listed. You'll need to \*\*mark each flagged issue as reviewed\*\* before you can proceed. - Expand the issues panel to see the full list. - Check off each issue once you've looked at it and decided what to do. ### 3. Confirm Your Setup Once all flagged issues are marked as reviewed, the \*\*Confirm\*\* button becomes active. Click it to finalize your documentation setup and move forward. > \*\*Note:\*\* You won't be able to confirm until every flagged issue has been reviewed. This is to make sure nothing is missed before your setup is saved. --- ## Rescanning a Repository If your repository has changed since the last scan, or if you want to re-run the discovery process, you can now trigger a rescan directly from the repository settings page. 1. Open the settings for your repository. 2. Click the \*\*Rescan\*\* button. 3. The scan will run in the background. A status indicator will update as it progresses. > \*\*Note:\*\* Rescanning will restart the onboarding status for that repository. You may be asked to go through the confirmation step again once the rescan completes. --- ## What to Expect - \*\*Confidence indicators\*\* are shown for automatically classified items, giving you a sense of how certain the system is about each classification. Items shown in red or amber may need your attention. - If the scan fails or is taking longer than expected, an error message will be displayed. You can try rescanning from the settings page. - Excluding an item only affects whether it's included in your documentation setup — it does not delete the file from your repository. - Document type changes and exclusions made on the confirmation screen are saved when you click \*\*Confirm\*\*. Navigating away before confirming will discard your changes.
